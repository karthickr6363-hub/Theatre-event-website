Grand Theatre

A Historic Venue for Unforgettable Performances

Theatre History

1895 - The Beginning

Grand Theatre was founded as a cultural beacon in the heart of the city, designed by renowned architect James Morrison. The original building featured ornate Victorian architecture and seated 800 patrons.

1920 - Expansion

Major renovation expanded the theatre to 1,200 seats, adding modern amenities while preserving the historic charm. The grand chandelier was installed, becoming an iconic symbol of the venue.

2000 - Modernization

State-of-the-art sound and lighting systems were installed, along with accessibility improvements. The theatre was designated as a historic landmark while embracing modern technology.

Today

Grand Theatre continues to be a premier destination for world-class performances, hosting over 200 shows annually and welcoming over 250,000 patrons each year.
